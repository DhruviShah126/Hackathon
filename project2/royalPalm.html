Royal Palm in 1901

In 1896-1897, Tuttle agreed to give Flagler some land so he could build his Royal Palm Hotel. With the construction of the hotel and a new railway station, the city of Miami began to grow into the metropolis it is today. In 1926 a hurricane severely damaged the hotel since it was constructed out of wood and not able to handle the strong winds. The hotel closed in 1928 after selling some of the hotel’s property to the city which greatly affected hotel management.

Royal Palm in 1927

The Tequesta (tuh-kes-tuh) were one of the first tribes in South Florida and they lived along the Miami River. When the Spanish invaded Florida, they were very guarded but eventually befriended the Spanish. The Spanish had little regard for the tribe and the two later groups had great animosity towards one another. The Tequesta began to die off of illness, settlement disputes, and Spanish treatment of the people and by the 1800s there were few Tequesta left. Though they inhabited the land before Miami was established as a city, they are still a vital part of the history.
