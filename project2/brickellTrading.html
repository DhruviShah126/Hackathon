The Brickell Family

Brickell Trading post was established by William and Mary Brickell. The resided along the Miami River around 1871 and purchased a lot of land from the surrounding area. They were quite successful in their trading with Miccosukee and Seminoles, but it wasn’t until Flagler began extending his railway that the couple seized an opportunity to give some of their land to Flagler, making their property very valuable. Mary Brickell eventually took control of the business and their wealth flourished, making the Brickell family one the founding families of Miami. Their impact can be seen all throughout the Brickell area today, where resides a bustling metropolis that people from all over the world go to visit.

The Brickell City Centre

Currently, Brickell is filled with high-rises, luxury apartments, and a bustling shopping/restaurant scene. As the financial district of South Florida, it is the center for Miami banking, investment, and finance. The center of the city is called Mary Brickell Village, after the woman who helped develop Brickell.
